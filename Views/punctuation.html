Comma(,):

A comma is a punctuation mark used to indicate a pause in a sentence, separate items in a list, or set off introductory or non-essential information.

Example:

I need to buy apples, bananas, and oranges.

Full Stop(.):

The full stop is a punctuation mark used to signify the end of a sentence or declarative statement.

Example:

The cat is sleeping.

Question Mark(?):

A question mark is a punctuation mark used to indicate the end of a sentence that asks a direct question.

Example:

Where are you going?

Dash(-):

A dash is a punctuation mark used to indicate a sudden break in thought or to set off an explanatory or parenthetical phrase.

Example:

She had one goalâ€”to win the race.

Exclamation(!):

An exclamation mark is a punctuation mark used to express strong emotion, surprise, or emphasis.

Example:

Congratulations on your graduation!

Colon(:):

A colon is a punctuation mark used to introduce a list, explanation, or quotation.

Example:

There are three primary colors: red, blue, and yellow.

Semicolon(;):

Definition: A semicolon is a punctuation mark used to link closely related independent clauses in a sentence.

Example:

She finished her work; then she went home.

Quotation Marks(" "):

Quotation marks are punctuation marks used to indicate direct speech or to enclose the titles of shorter works.

Example:

She said, "I'll be there in five minutes.

Apostrophe('):

An apostrophe is a punctuation mark used to indicate possession or to show the omission of letters in contractions.

Example:

Who's at the door?

Parentheses(()):

Parentheses are punctuation marks used to enclose additional information that is not essential to the main point of the sentence.

Example:

The concert (which lasted three hours) was amazing.
