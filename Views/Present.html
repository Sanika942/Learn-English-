Present Tense

Simple Present:

The Simple Present tense is a verb form used to describe actions that occur regularly, habits, general truths, and scheduled events.

Formula:

S+v1+O

Example

I play Tennis.


Present Continuous:

The Present Continuous tense is a verb form used to describe actions that are currently happening, ongoing, or in progress at the time of speaking.

Formula:

S+am/is/are+v4+O

Example

I am playing Tennis.


Present Perfect:

The Present Perfect tense is a verb form used to express actions that were completed at an unspecified time in the past or actions that started in the past and have a connection to the present.

Formula:

S+have/has+v3+O

Example

I have played Tennis.


Present Perfect Continuous:

The Present Perfect Continuous tense is a verb form used to express actions that started in the past and are still ongoing, or actions that have just been completed and have a connection to the present, emphasizing their duration.

Formula:

S+have/has+been+v4+O

Example

I have been playing Tennis.
